About me

A designer who asks
why before asking how

I believe great UX is not about beautiful screens — it's about removing the distance between what people need and what technology provides.

👩🏽‍💻

Hi, I'm Estefany

I'm a UX designer specialising in AI-powered products, with a particular obsession with the moment when a person first meets an intelligent system — and what happens to their trust from that point forward.

My path into design started with a simple frustration: I kept encountering software that felt like it was designed for the engineers who built it, not the people who had to use it. So I switched sides. Since then I've worked across fintech, health tech, and enterprise AI — always asking the same question: whose mental model does this interface actually match?

What makes AI UX different — and endlessly interesting — is that the system itself is uncertain. It doesn't always know the right answer, and neither does the user. Designing for that mutual uncertainty, in a way that still feels confident and safe, is the challenge I show up for every day.

What I believe

  • 01

    Clarity over cleverness

    Complexity is not sophistication. The most powerful designs are the ones where users never notice the design at all.

  • 02

    Trust is designed, not assumed

    With AI especially, every output, every explanation, every error message is either building or eroding user confidence. Design for trust deliberately.

  • 03

    Research before wireframes

    A beautiful screen that solves the wrong problem is still a failure. Understanding the problem space thoroughly is not optional — it's the work.

  • 04

    Inclusive by default

    Designing for edge cases — cognitive load, low vision, non-native language — almost always improves the experience for everyone.
