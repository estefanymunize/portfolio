About me

A designer who asks
why before asking how

Great UX isn't about beautiful screens — it's about removing the distance between what people need and what products actually give them.

Hi, I'm Estefany

I'm a UX designer with a genuine obsession for the moment when a product finally makes sense to someone, that instant of clarity when friction disappears and things just work.

My path into design started with a simple frustration: I kept encountering software that felt like it was designed for the engineers who built it, not the people who had to use it. So I switched sides.

I'm also building skills in AI and emerging technology design, because I believe the next wave of products will need designers who understand both human behaviour and how intelligent systems work. But the foundation is always the same: understand the person first.

What I believe

  • 01

    Clarity over cleverness

    Complexity is not sophistication. The most powerful designs are the ones where users never notice the design at all.

  • 02

    Trust is designed, not assumed

    With AI especially, every output, every explanation, every error message is either building or eroding user confidence. Design for trust deliberately.

  • 03

    Research before wireframes

    A beautiful screen that solves the wrong problem is still a failure. Understanding the problem space thoroughly is not optional — it's the work.

  • 04

    Inclusive by default

    Designing for edge cases — cognitive load, low vision, non-native language — almost always improves the experience for everyone.
